Harm Happens Silently — and Often Preventably

Building Collapse – Nairobi, Jan 2026

On January 2, 2026, a multi-storey building under construction in South C collapsed, killing at least two people and trapping others under rubble. Illegal extra floors, poor supervision, and regulatory failures turned a site into tragedy — fully preventable with proper safety systems and oversight.

Biometric Data Breach – Worldcoin Kenya

Following a 2025 High Court ruling declaring iris scan collection unlawful, Worldcoin permanently deleted all Kenyan biometric data in early 2026 under ODPC supervision. The case exposed thousands to privacy risks and identity threats due to inadequate consent and safeguards.

Loan Apps Data Abuse – Kenya

Digital lenders like Whitepath and Mulla Pride faced millions in ODPC fines for unlawfully accessing contacts/photos and using third-party data for harassment and shaming. These breaches left vulnerable borrowers exposed to fraud, blackmail, and severe privacy violations.

Ongoing Pollution – Nairobi River

Despite the 2017 plastic ban, Nairobi River remains heavily polluted with plastic waste, toxic heavy metals (chromium, lead), and industrial effluents. This causes flooding, disease, and long-term health damage — a silent crisis that continues due to poor enforcement and waste management failures.
